Overview

This analysis compares Promise Health Plan-negotiated rates at SC hospitals to Medicare benchmarks using Machine-Readable Files (MRFs) submitted under the CMS Hospital Price Transparency Rule (45 CFR Part 180). Rates are expressed as % of Medicare, utilization-weighted using Komodo Health national claims frequency (330M covered lives). Files downloaded April 21, 2026.

APC Caveat (McLeod Health System): McLeod Health hospitals publish outpatient standard charges at the Ambulatory Payment Classification (APC) group level rather than individual CPT/HCPCS procedure code level. APCs are CMS payment groupings for the Outpatient Prospective Payment System (OPPS) that consolidate multiple related procedures into a single bundled code. The Komodo Region 5 benchmark table used in this analysis operates at the individual CPT/HCPCS code level. APC codes cannot be matched to individual Komodo benchmarks, producing severely limited match rates for McLeod outpatient and professional data (~12,000 records vs 200,000+ for comparable Prisma hospitals). McLeod inpatient data (MS-DRG) is not affected and matches normally.

Methodology

Code Resolution (cascade)

  • code|1: Used if type = MS-DRG, CPT, or HCPCS
  • code|2: Fallback for CDM rows (94% of CDM rows have CPT/HCPCS in code|2)
  • code|3, code|4: Additional fallbacks

Dollar Rate Resolution

  • Use standard_charge|negotiated_dollar directly when populated
  • Otherwise: rate = negotiated_percentage x gross_charge

Medicare Benchmark Matching

Each code + section combination matched against Komodo Region 5 reference (6,141 codes):

  • Inpatient: MS-DRG codes matched to IPPS DRG weight x base rate
  • Outpatient: CPT/HCPCS codes matched to OPPS APC payment amounts
  • Professional: CPT codes matched to PFS locality-adjusted physician fee schedule

% of Medicare Calculation

pct_medicare = negotiated_dollar / matched_medicare_benchmark x 100. Outlier cap: rows where pct > 600% excluded.

Komodo Utilization Weighting

Each code weighted by claims_per_1000 from Komodo Health (330M covered lives, Nov 2025). Weighted average = Sum(pct x claims_per_1000) / Sum(claims_per_1000).

Cohort Aggregation

Aggregate views weight each hospital by included row count. "Combined" mixes Prisma and Non-Prisma. Columbia and Greenville refer to target MSA groupings as assigned in the pipeline.

Limitations

Professional Bucket Caveat

Professional benchmarks use the PFS locality schedule (physician billing). Hospitals that bill facility-level charges under professional codes will show inflated Professional % values. Inpatient and Outpatient are the cleaner buckets.

Code Coverage

Komodo Region 5 reference: 6,141 codes. Hospital MRFs: 15,000-25,000 line items. Unmatched rows: hospital-specific CDM codes, pharmacy/drug codes (NDC), revenue codes without CPT, and low-volume specialty procedures.

Outpatient Drugs / Imaging Outliers

Outpatient Drugs and Imaging show very high % of Medicare (300-2,500%+). Hospital outpatient drug charges vs. very low per-unit Medicare drug payments. Directionally meaningful but not literal multipliers.

Interpretation Guide

  • 100% of Medicare = payer pays exactly what Medicare pays
  • <150% = strong payer leverage or value-based arrangement
  • 150-200% = typical mid-range commercial contract
  • 200-300% = aggressive pricing, high-leverage system
  • >300% = premium contract or facility billing under physician fee schedule

Prisma vs Non-Prisma

Promise rates at Prisma hospitals (160-220% inpatient) reflect pricing power of a dominant integrated system. Non-Prisma (McLeod) inpatient rates higher in most categories, suggesting less leverage with McLeod.
